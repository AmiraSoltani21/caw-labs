Since October 7, 2023, the Gaza Strip has been enduring one of the most devastating military campaigns in modern history. According to daily reports from the Gaza Ministry of Health, nearly 47,107 martyrs and 111,147 wounded have been recorded as of early 2025. Independent databases estimate that more than 60,199 people have been killed, including 10,656 children and 10,271 women.


Among the victims, children and women have borne the heaviest toll. . Approximately 33.1% of those killed are children (ages 0–17), and 18.3% are women. Entire families have been wiped out, and many mothers and caregivers were killed while trying to protect or rescue their children under continuous bombardment.

The material and infrastructural damage is catastrophic. Nearly 80% of homes have been damaged or destroyed, 68% of the road network has been rendered unusable, and more than 50% of hospitals are only partially functional. Businesses, schools, and places of worship have been leveled, leaving Gaza in a state of complete humanitarian collapse
. Water, electricity, and medical services have nearly ceased to function, and access to food and aid remains critically limited.
